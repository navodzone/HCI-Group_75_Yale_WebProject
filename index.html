About Yale

The Yale School of Art is a graduate school that confers MFAs in Graphic Design, Painting/Printmaking, Photography, and Sculpture and offers undergraduate-level art courses to Yale College students.

The Courses We Offer

Graphic Design

This course combines creative ability with expertise in computer technology to develop excellent communication skills in graphic design and multimedia.

Painting

Enrolling in a course in painting can be a great way to refine skills or experience a brand new medium. Learn by Doing actual paintings and assignments.

Photography

This course contains both practical and theoretical subjects regarding Digital Photography and the course covers the module of marketing and business management in the Photography Industry.

Sculpture

Courses are available for both experienced and budding beginner sculptors. Learn sculpting techniques with experienced tutors and discover the joy of being creative.

Yale School of Art


The Yale School of Art is the art school of Yale University. Founded in 1869 as the first professional fine arts school in the United States, it grants Masters of Fine Arts degrees to students completing a two-year course in graphic design, painting/printmaking, photography, or sculpture.
